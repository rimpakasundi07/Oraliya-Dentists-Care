What is a Dental Filling?

A Dental Filling, also known as a Tooth Filling or Cavity Filling, is a restorative dental treatment used to repair teeth damaged due to tooth decay, cavities, fractures, or wear.

At ORALIYA, dental fillings are performed using advanced restorative materials that not only repair the tooth but also restore its strength, shape, and natural appearance.

Dental fillings help

  • Stop further decay
  • Protect the inner tooth structure
  • Improve chewing and biting function
  • Maintain natural tooth integrity

A dental filling seals the damaged area and prevents bacteria from spreading deeper into the tooth.

Why Do Teeth Need Dental Fillings?

Tooth structure can get damaged due to

Tooth decay (cavities)
Dental trauma or accidents
Teeth grinding (bruxism)
Nail biting
Chipped or cracked teeth
Old or broken fillings

| Types of Dental Fillings at ORALIYA

Dental fillings are selected based on cavity size, location, aesthetics, durability needs, and patient preference.

Composite Fillings (Tooth-Coloured Fillings)

Composite fillings are the most preferred and recommended dental fillings at ORALIYA.

Material: Resin + Plastic Appearance: Matches natural tooth color

Benefits

  • Natural-looking & aesthetic
  • Bonds strongly with tooth
  • Preserves more structure
  • Suitable for front & back teeth
  • Long-lasting (10–15 years with proper care)

Composite material is also used for

  • Broken teeth repair
  • Chipped teeth
  • Crowns
  • Inlays & Onlays
  • Veneers

Best choice for patients who want invisible restorations.

Silver Amalgam Fillings

Silver amalgam fillings contain:

  • Silver
  • Tin
  • Copper
  • Zinc
  • Liquid Mercury (Elemental)

These fillings are now less commonly used due to

  • Metallic appearance
  • Mercury content concerns
  • Unsuitability for patients with metal allergies

At ORALIYA, we usually recommend safer and more aesthetic alternatives.

Ceramic Fillings (Porcelain Fillings)

Ceramic fillings are made of high-quality porcelain and are known for strength and aesthetics.

Advantages

  • Tooth-colored & natural-looking 
  • Highly stain-resistant 
  • Durable and long-lasting
  • Ideal for large cavities

Ceramic fillings cost slightly more due to

  • Lab fabrication
  • Premium material quality
  • Superior aesthetics

GIC (Glass Ionomer Cement) Fillings

Recommended for

  • Baby teeth
  • Temporary fillings
  • Copper
  • Cavities near gum line
  • Pediatric dentistry

Key Benefit

  • Releases fluoride to prevent further decay

Drawback

  • Less durable than composite or ceramic fillings

A dentist at ORALIYA will confirm the need for filling after a clinical examination and X-ray.

When Do You Need a Dental Filling?

You may require a dental filling if you notice

Visible hole or pit in tooth
Toothache or pain while chewing
Black or brown spots on tooth
Sensitivity to hot, cold, or sweet food
Food getting stuck between teeth
Broken, chipped, or worn teeth
Single or multiple cavities

| Dental Filling Procedure
at ORALIYA – Step by Step

Tooth Examination

The dentist examines your tooth and checks for pain, sensitivity, discoloration, or decay. X-rays may be taken if required.

X-Ray & Material Selection

  • If decay has reached the nerve → Root Canal Treatment (RCT)
  • If decay is limited → Dental Filling

The dentist discusses the best filling material for your case.

Removal of Tooth Decay

  • Decayed portion removed using dental instruments
  • High-speed precision drilling ensures complete decay removal

 Filling Placement

The selected filling material (Composite, GIC, Ceramic, etc.) is placed into the cavity. For ceramic fillings, impressions are taken and sent to the lab (inlays/onlays).

Setting & Hardening

  • Composite fillings hardened using curing light
  • Other materials set naturally
  • Dentist advises on eating time

Bite Adjustment

Articulating paper is used to check bite pressure and ensure the filling feels comfortable during chewing.
